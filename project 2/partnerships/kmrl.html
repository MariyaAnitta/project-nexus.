KMRL

  • General Info about KMRL

    Kochi Metro Rail Limited (KMRL) is a government-owned company tasked with the implementation, operation, and maintenance of the Kochi Metro, a rapid transit system serving the city of Kochi in Kerala, India. Established in 2011, KMRL operates as a joint venture between the Government of India and the Government of Kerala, with both entities holding equal stakes. The company's headquarters are located in Kochi, reflecting its close ties to the community it serves. The primary objective of KMRL is to develop a world-class metro system that enhances the quality of life for Kochi’s residents by providing a reliable, safe, and efficient public transportation system. This initiative aims to alleviate traffic congestion in the city while promoting sustainable urban mobility and contributing to Kochi's economic development. The vision of KMRL encompasses the creation of a transit network that is both accessible and environmentally sustainable. The Kochi Metro network includes elevated and at-grade sections, with stations that are equipped with modern amenities. The initial phase of the metro system runs from Aluva to Petta, covering approximately 25 kilometers and featuring several strategically placed stations. Future expansion plans aim to extend the network to more areas within the city and its suburbs, ensuring comprehensive connectivity for residents and visitors alike. Rajagiri School of Engineering and Technology (RSET) has partnered with Kochi Metro Rail Limited (KMRL) in various collaborative efforts aimed at enhancing urban mobility and fostering innovation in public transportation. This partnership leverages the technical expertise and research capabilities of RSET to support KMRL's mission of providing efficient and sustainable transit solutions in Kochi.
